Why we named it Gamsa

S Sarah, Founder ·Mar 18, 2026 ·6 min read

감The first Korean word a lot of us learn isn't hello or goodbye — it's 감사, gamsa: gratitude. It's what you say before a meal, after a kindness, at the end of a long day. When we set out to build a breakfast brand, that was the only name that felt honest.

Because this whole thing started with a feeling at the table. Growing up, breakfast wasn't cereal and a rush out the door. It was something savory and warm — often a bowl of juk (죽), the rice porridge my mom and grandmother would start before the rest of us were awake.

The breakfast I missed

Years later, mornings looked different — louder, faster, eaten standing up. The sweet, sugary breakfasts on every shelf never sat right. What I actually wanted was the bowl I grew up with, but I never had the 30-plus minutes it takes to soak rice and slow-simmer it into something soft and comforting.

"It tastes just like the rice porridge my mom and grandma used to make." — and that's exactly the point.

So we spent a long time in a small NYC kitchen working backwards from that memory. How do you keep the savory depth — garlic, sesame, miso, tamari, a whisper of seaweed — and the homestyle comfort, but get it down to two and a half minutes and a packet you can throw in a bag?

Made simple, not cheap

We make it in small batches, not big factories, so we can keep quality front and center. It costs a little more than the sweet stuff — and we think that's fair for real ingredients and a recipe that took years to get right. The best part is what people do with it: crack in an egg, add a spoonful of kimchi, finish with chili crisp. Make it yours.

That's the gratitude we built into the name. Gratitude for the people who cooked for us, for the mornings we get to slow down, and for everyone now starting their day with a bowl. 감사합니다 — thank you.
